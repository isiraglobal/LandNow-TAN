Why Land & Water

Two assets. One thesis.

Why land and water together are the most defensible position in natural capital.

Land: the underlying collateral of civilization.

Land has no counterparty risk. It cannot be delisted, diluted, taken private, or rug-pulled. Every form of economic activity ultimately rests on a piece of ground. The oldest form of wealth is also the most resilient.

What has changed is the accessibility of land as an institutional-grade asset. Fractional ownership, syndication structures, and water-right unbundling have opened the door for networked capital to operate at scale.

Water: the most under-priced asset in America.

Water rights in the western US are priced as agricultural inputs when they function as structural leverage on the most supply-constrained resource on earth. A senior water right on a river with junior municipal users below you is a toll booth. Every year, the toll gets more valuable.

The mismatch between current pricing and intrinsic value is where LandNow members operate. This is not a prediction. It is a structural reality of hydrology, law, and demographics.

The convergence of scarcity and structure. When water rights appreciate faster than the land they sit on. The convergence of scarcity and structure. When water rights appreciate faster than the land they sit on.
